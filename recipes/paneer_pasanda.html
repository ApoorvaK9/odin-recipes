Paneer Pasanda

Description:

A rich and flavorful North Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) stuffed with a spiced filling and cooked in a creamy gravy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts (soaked in warm water)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix khoya, chopped nuts, raisins, cardamom powder, saffron strands (if using), salt, fresh cream, and chopped coriander leaves.
  2. Cut the paneer into slices of about 1/4 inch thickness.
  3. Carefully slit each slice horizontally, making sure not to cut all the way through, creating a pocket.
  4. Stuff each pocket with the prepared filling.
  5. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  6. Carefully place the stuffed paneer slices in the pan and shallow fry until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Remove and set aside.
  8. Blend the soaked cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste.
  9. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  10. Add cumin seeds, cardamom pods, and bay leaf. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  11. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  12. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  13. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  14. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  15. Add the cashew paste and cook for a few minutes, stirring continuously.
  16. Add water and bring the gravy to a simmer. Cook until it thickens slightly.
  17. Stir in the fresh cream and garam masala powder. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  18. Check for seasoning and adjust salt if needed.
  19. Gently place the shallow-fried paneer sandwiches into the gravy.
  20. Let them simmer in the gravy for 5-7 minutes so they absorb the flavors.
  21. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  22. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
